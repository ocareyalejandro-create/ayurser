## The governing principle (this is the engine)

> **"Equal qualities lead to increase; opposing qualities lead to decrease."**
> — *AH Su. 1.13½*

Everything the app recommends flows from this one law. A quality you already have
*too much of* is aggravated by more of the same and **pacified by its opposite.**
Example from the text: a dry (Vata) person in dry, cold weather grows drier — and
oil softens it back. (*AH Su. 1.13½*)

So the honest logic is not "what's your type" but: **which qualities are running
high today → offer their opposites.** This is the real basis for "Ground / Cool /
Stir," and it replaces any arbitrary scoring.

## The three doshas

Three forces (*tridoṣa*): **Vāta, Pitta, Kapha**. *"Perfect balance of the three
leads to health; imbalance leads to disease."* (*AH Su. 1.5½–6½*)

### Vāta — the **wind** (air + ether)
Qualities (*guṇa*), *AH Su. 1.10½*: **rūkṣa** dry · **laghu** light · **śīta** cold ·
**khara** rough · **sūkṣma** subtle · **cala** moving/changeable.
- *Runs high as:* dryness, cold hands, restlessness, anxiety, irregular digestion/sleep, gas.
- *Pacify with the opposite:* warmth, moisture/oil, weight, steadiness, routine.

### Pitta — the **fire** (fire + a little water)
Qualities, *AH Su. 1.11*: **sasneha** slightly oily · **tīkṣṇa** sharp/penetrating ·
**uṣṇa** hot · **laghu** light · **sara** flowing · **drava** liquid.
- *Runs high as:* heat, sharpness, irritability, inflammation, loose/burning digestion.
- *Pacify with the opposite:* cooling, calm, sweetness, shade, less spice & intensity.

### Kapha — the **earth** (earth + water)
Qualities, *AH Su. 1.12*: **snigdha** oily · **śīta** cold · **guru** heavy ·
**manda** slow/mild · **ślakṣṇa** smooth · **sthira** stable.
- *Runs high as:* heaviness, sluggishness, congestion, lethargy, slow digestion, holding on.
- *Pacify with the opposite:* lightness, warmth, stimulation, movement, less heavy/sweet.

*(The 20 guṇas come in ten opposed pairs — heavy/light, slow/sharp, cold/hot,
oily/dry, smooth/rough, solid/liquid, soft/hard, stable/mobile, subtle/gross,
non-slimy/slimy — AH Su. 1.18. This pairing is the whole toolkit.)*

## Prakriti vs Vikriti — nature vs weather

- **Prakriti** (constitution): inherent from conception, set by the qualities of
  sperm & ovum; single-dosha, dual-dosha, or balanced (*sama*, considered best).
  (*AH Su. 1.9–10*) — **fixed; not honestly pinned by a quick quiz.**
- **Vikriti** (current state): the *imbalance showing up now*. This is what daily
  check-ins read.

This is the textual ground for our **"weather, not labels"** stance: lead with
Vikriti (today), let Prakriti emerge slowly.

## Agni — the digestive fire

Four states (*AH Su. 1.7½*):
- **Viṣama** (Vāta) — irregular: appetite high then low.
- **Tīkṣṇa** (Pitta) — sharp: strong, sometimes burning, appetite.
- **Manda** (Kapha) — dull: low, slow digestion.
- **Sama** — balanced: appetite and digestion steady, at the right time.

Agni is the doorway concept for *digestion* guidance — introduced in context, not up front.

## The doshas move through the day (validates time-awareness)

The doshas dominate by part of the day, life, and digestion (*AH Su. 1.6½–7½*):
- **Day & night:** first part **Kapha** (morning), middle **Pitta** (midday), last **Vāta** (evening).
- **Life:** childhood Kapha, adulthood Pitta, old age Vāta.
- **After eating:** Kapha → Pitta → Vāta across digestion.

→ Time-aware guidance isn't a gimmick; it's classical. A morning check-in and an
evening one can honestly differ.

## Taste (rasa) → dosha — for food guidance

Six tastes (*AH Su. 1.13½–14*): sweet (*madhura*), sour (*amla*), salt (*lavaṇa*),
bitter (*tikta*), pungent (*kaṭu*), astringent (*kaṣāya*).
- **Sweet, sour, salt** → pacify **Vāta**, increase **Kapha**.
- **Bitter, pungent, astringent** → pacify **Kapha**, increase **Vāta**.
- **Astringent, bitter, sweet** → pacify **Pitta**; sour, salt, pungent increase it.

(Potency *vīrya*: astringent/bitter/sweet tend cooling; sour/salt/pungent tend heating. *AH Su. 1*)

## Dinacharya (daily routine) + the dosha clock

### The dosha clock — the doshas turn through the day
The classical day-cycle (*AH Su. 1.6½–7½*) is given practical clock-times by Lad
(*Self-Healing*, Ch. XI "Time," pp. 104–105). The two agree: a day is two full
turns of Kapha → Pitta → Vāta.

| Window | Dosha runs high | What it feels like → what it asks for |
|---|---|---|
| **~Sunrise–10 am** | **Kapha** | Heavy, steady, a little slow. *Move, lighten, don't linger in bed.* Best window for activity and exercise. |
| **~10 am–2 pm** | **Pitta** | Hunger and heat peak; digestion strongest. *Eat the main meal at midday* (Lad: lunch ~10–11 am onward, "when the sun is up"). |
| **~2 pm–sunset** | **Vāta** | Light, active, supple, but prone to scatter. *Steady, warm, ground as the day tips toward evening.* |
| **~6 pm–10 pm** | **Kapha** | Cool air, settling, inertia. *Wind down; light early dinner before sunset; bed before 10.* |
| **~10 pm–2 am** | **Pitta** | Food digested, body repairs. *Be asleep* — staying up here "completely changes the body chemistry." |
| **~2 am–sunrise** | **Vāta** | Movement returns; we naturally wake and excrete. *Rise near the end of this window* (classical **Brahma Muhūrta**, ~45 min before sunrise — *AH Su. 2.1*). |

→ Time-aware guidance is classical, not a gimmick. A morning check-in (Kapha-leaning
hours) and an evening one (Kapha settling again) can honestly differ.

### The morning sequence (dinacharya)
Distilled from **AH Su. Ch. 2 (Dinacharyā)** — public-domain classical text — and
Lad, *Self-Healing*, Ch. X "Lifestyle and Routine" (pp. 100–103). They overlap closely.

1. **Wake before sunrise** — at Brahma Muhūrta. (*AH Su. 2.1*; Lad p. 100–101: "awaken before sunrise.")
2. **Eliminate** — empty bowels and bladder on waking. (*AH Su. 2*; Lad p. 101.)
3. **Clean the mouth & scrape the tongue** — brush, then scrape the tongue (Lad: a metal/silver scraper). The classical text uses bitter/astringent/pungent twigs. (*AH Su. 2.2–3*; Lad p. 100.) *Tongue scraping clears the night's coating (āma) and lightly stimulates the organs.*
4. **Oil pulling / gargle (gandūṣa)** — hold/swish warm water, milk, decoction, **or herbal oil** in the mouth. (*AH Su. 2.6.*) This is the classical root of "oil pulling."
5. **Warm water** — a glass of warm water to wake digestion and the bowels. (Lad p. 100.)
6. **Abhyaṅga (oil self-massage), then bathe** — see the next section. (*AH Su. 2.8–9*; Lad p. 100.)
7. **Movement, then breath/meditation** — exercise to *half strength* for Vāta/Pitta types, fuller for Kapha (*AH Su. 2.10*); breath-work and sitting after. *(The breath/movement how-to is Vanessa's domain — see that section.)*
8. **Eat with the clock** — main meal at midday (Pitta time); light dinner before sunset; bed before 10 pm. (Lad pp. 101, 104.)

**Small daily anchors (Lad, pp. 101–102), good for everyone:**
- A short walk (~15 min) after meals.
- Eat in silence, slowly, with attention — not at a screen.
- Don't drown digestion: sip, don't gulp water with meals; "do not eat unless hungry, do not drink unless thirsty."
- **Sesame oil on the soles of the feet at night** calms the mind and gives quiet sleep — a tiny, low-effort Vāta-pacifying ritual. (Lad p. 102.)

## Oils / abhyanga by dosha

> **Abhyaṅga** = daily oil self-massage. The classical charter (*AH Su. 2.8–9*):
> done daily, in the morning — it *"delays ageing, relieves tiredness and excess of
> Vāta, improves vision, nourishes the tissues, prolongs life, gives good sleep and
> better skin."* Apply **especially to the ears, head, and feet.** **Avoid** when Kapha
> is high (congestion, heaviness), during indigestion, or just after a cleanse.
> (Public-domain classical source — paraphrased.)

So oiling is, by default, the great **Vāta-pacifier**: warm oil is the exact opposite
of Vāta's *dry, cold, rough, mobile* qualities. The oil and the timing shift by dosha
(oil type per Lad, *Self-Healing*, Ch. XII-E "Massage," p. 128):

| Dosha running high | Oil | Touch | When (dosha clock) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Vāta** (dry, cold, rough) | **Sesame** — warming, heavy, deeply nourishing. Warm it first. | *Gentle*, slow. Stroke against the grain of the hair to open the dry, closed follicles (Lad p. 128). | **Evening** especially (Lad: "oil massage in the evening for vāta"); soles of feet at bedtime for sleep. Also fine on rising. |
| **Pitta** (hot, sharp) | **Sunflower** or **sandalwood** — cooling (Lad p. 128). **Coconut** is the common cooling choice in modern practice *(confirm with practitioner — Lad names sunflower/sandalwood, not coconut)*. | *Gentle*, unhurried. | **Afternoon** (Pitta time); avoid the heat of midday. |
| **Kapha** (heavy, cold, oily, stable) | **Light** oil, sparingly — Lad names **corn** or **calamus-root** oil. Kapha already has its own oiliness, so **less oil, or none.** | *Vigorous, deep.* When skipping oil, use a **dry powder rub (udvartana)** or dry-brushing — classically this *"calms aggravated Kapha, burns fat, and clears the skin"* (*AH Su. 2.15*). | **Morning** (Kapha time) — to break heaviness and get moving. |

*Honest caveat:* the **oil choices** (sesame / sunflower / dry-brush) are from Lad and
the classical texts; **coconut for Pitta** and **dry-brushing as the default Kapha
practice** are widespread in modern Ayurveda and consistent with the cooling/lightening
logic, but should be *(confirmed with a practitioner)* before we present them as
classical. The governing law is the safe ground: **warm oil opposes Vāta's dryness;
cooling oil opposes Pitta's heat; little-or-no oil opposes Kapha's heaviness.**

## Food & lifestyle by dosha

> Built from the rasa→doṣa logic above (*AH Su. 1.13½–14*) and Lad's **Table 5 —
> "Food Guidelines for Basic Constitutional Types"** (*Self-Healing*, pp. 82–83),
> Chapter VIII "Diet" (pp. 80–81), and Table 6 "Tastes and Their Actions" (pp. 90–91).
> Lad is paraphrased, never copied. **Read favor/avoid as "today's weather," not a
> permanent diet** — it's the qualities running high that we're answering, per our
> "weather, not labels" stance.

**The shared principle (read this first).** Food carries qualities just like weather
does. *Equal qualities increase; opposites pacify* (*AH Su. 1.13½*). So:
- **how** a food is prepared can matter as much as **what** it is — *raw, cold, dry*
  preparation adds Vāta qualities; *warm, cooked, moist, oily* preparation removes them.
- Lad's own table flags this directly: raw vegetables are listed under **"NO"** for
  Vāta, with the footnote that they are *"OK in moderation, with oil dressing"* (p. 83).

### Vāta — the **wind** (dry · cold · light · rough · mobile)
**Needs the opposite: WARM · COOKED · MOIST · OILY · grounding · steady.**

> ⚠️ **Correction baked in:** Vāta is pacified by warm, cooked, moist, oily food and
> **aggravated by raw, cold, dry food.** A cold raw cucumber on a windy, scattered
> (Vāta) morning is the *wrong* call — its cold quality feeds the cold. (Lad lists
> cucumber as Vāta-relieving *as a substance* — sweet, juicy — but **served raw and
> cold it adds the very qualities Vāta is drowning in**; if used for Vāta, it should be
> cooked/warm and oiled. Table 7, p. 96.) For a Vāta morning, favor warm cooked grains,
> soups, stewed fruit, warm milk — not a cold salad.

- **Favor** (Lad Table 5, pp. 82–83): cooked vegetables — asparagus, beets, carrots,
  sweet potato, cooked onion, okra, zucchini, green beans (warm, with a little oil);
  sweet juicy fruit — banana, avocado, mango, sweet berries, soaked dried fruit, stewed
  apple/pear (not raw-dry); cooked oats, rice, wheat; warm milk, ghee; sesame and other
  oils; warming spices — ginger, cumin (Lad's "ginger + a pinch of salt" appetizer,
  p. 101). Tastes that pacify Vāta: **sweet, sour, salt** (*AH Su. 1.14*).
- **Avoid / reduce** (Lad, pp. 82–83): **raw vegetables**, broccoli, cabbage,
  cauliflower, sprouts (raw/cold/rough); **dry fruit, raw apples, cranberries, pears,
  pomegranate, watermelon**; dry/light grains — barley, buckwheat, corn, millet, dry
  oats, rye; cold drinks, iced food, leftovers. Tastes that *aggravate* Vāta:
  **bitter, pungent, astringent** (drying) (*AH Su. 1.14*).
- *Lifestyle:* warmth, oil, routine, early steady meals, enough rest — wind needs walls.

### Pitta — the **fire** (hot · sharp · light · slightly oily · flowing)
**Needs the opposite: COOLING · sweet · bitter · astringent · calm · not too oily/spicy.**

- **Favor** (Lad Table 5; Diet p. 80): sweet & bitter vegetables — cucumber, leafy
  greens, broccoli, asparagus, cabbage, cauliflower, green beans, sweet potato, zucchini;
  sweet ripe fruit — apples, ripe mango, sweet grapes, melons, pears, sweet
  pineapple, pomegranate; cooling grains — barley, basmati/white rice, oats, wheat;
  cooling dairy in moderation; coriander, fennel, mint. Tastes that pacify Pitta:
  **sweet, bitter, astringent** (cooling) (*AH Su. 1.14*).
- **Avoid / reduce** (Lad p. 80): hot, spicy, pungent and sour foods — chili, garlic,
  raw onion, hot peppers; sour fruit — lemons, sour grapes, sour pineapple; fermented
  and very salty food; alcohol; tomatoes, eggplant (heating). Tastes that aggravate
  Pitta: **sour, salt, pungent** (heating) (*AH Su. 1.14*).
- *Lifestyle:* cool and calm — shade, moonlight, the afternoon for hard work not the
  midday heat; don't skip meals (a hungry Pitta gets sharp).

### Kapha — the **earth** (heavy · cold · oily · slow · smooth · stable)
**Needs the opposite: LIGHT · WARM · DRY · well-spiced · stimulating.**

- **Favor** (Lad Table 5): pungent & bitter vegetables — asparagus, broccoli,
  cabbage, cauliflower, leafy greens, onion, garlic, peppers, radish, sprouts; light
  drying fruit — apples, cranberries, dry figs, pomegranate, dried fruit; light grains —
  barley, cooked oats, millet, dry oats, rye, small basmati; legumes; plenty of warming
  spices — ginger, black pepper, turmeric, chili. Tastes that pacify Kapha:
  **pungent, bitter, astringent** (*AH Su. 1.14*).
- **Avoid / reduce** (Lad; Diet p. 80): heavy, oily, cold, sweet, dense food — dairy,
  ice cream, cheese, yogurt, bananas, coconut, dates, sweet juicy melons, wheat in
  excess, fried food, excess salt and sweets, cold drinks. **Kapha is the one type that
  benefits from skipping breakfast** (eating in Kapha time adds Kapha — Lad p. 104).
  Tastes that aggravate Kapha: **sweet, sour, salt** (*AH Su. 1.14*).
- *Lifestyle:* movement, warmth, dryness, stimulation; rise early, don't nap after
  meals (a daytime nap "increases Kapha," Lad p. 102).

### Seasonal note (everyone — the weather outside is weather inside)
The same like-increases-like law runs through the year (Lad, "Time," p. 105; "Diet,"
p. 81): **autumn** is dry and windy → Vāta rises → warm, moist, grounding food;
**summer** is hot → Pitta rises → cooling food, avoid hot/spicy/sour; **winter / early
spring** is cold and damp → Kapha rises → light, warm, dry, well-spiced food, avoid
cold drinks and heavy dairy.

## Mixed states — honest overlaps

> When two doshas run high together, the honest move is **not** to average them and
> **not** to pick a side. It is to find the food and habits that **pacify both without
> aggravating either** — the overlap of their two "favor" lists, avoiding anything that
> calms one by feeding the other. This is just the governing law (*AH Su. 1.13½*) applied
> twice at once. The pairs below follow directly from the per-dosha lists above; the
> *specific menu calls* are reasoned synthesis and worth a practitioner's eye
> *(confirm with practitioner)*.

### Vāta + Pitta — *warm-not-hot, moist, mildly sweet, cooked*
The trap: Vāta wants **warm**, Pitta wants **cool** — opposite temperatures.
- **The overlap that serves both:** **sweet, mildly cooling, moist, cooked** food at a
  **warm (not hot, not iced)** temperature. Sweet taste pacifies *both* (*AH Su. 1.14*).
- **Favor:** cooked sweet grains (rice, oats, wheat), stewed sweet fruit, sweet
  cooked vegetables (zucchini, asparagus, sweet potato), a little ghee, coriander/fennel/mint.
- **Avoid both errors:** ❌ **raw, cold, dry** food (aggravates Vāta) and ❌
  **hot, spicy, sour, fermented** food (aggravates Pitta). So: not a cold salad, *and*
  not a chili. Warm, soft, gently sweet, cooked.

### Pitta + Kapha — *warm, light, dry, bitter & astringent, not heavy*
The trap: Pitta wants **cooling**, Kapha wants **warming** — opposite temperatures.
- **The overlap that serves both:** **bitter and astringent** tastes pacify *both*
  (*AH Su. 1.14*); both want food that is **light, not heavy** and **not oily**.
- **Favor:** leafy greens and bitter vegetables, broccoli, cauliflower, asparagus,
  light grains (barley, basmati), beans; gentle warming spices used lightly (Kapha needs
  some heat; Pitta can't take much) — coriander, fennel, a little fresh ginger or turmeric.
- **Avoid both errors:** ❌ **hot, spicy, sour, salty** food (aggravates Pitta) and ❌
  **heavy, cold, oily, sweet, dense** food — cheese, ice cream, fried food (aggravates Kapha).
- *On temperature:* aim warm-but-not-hot, and keep it **light and dry** — lightness is
  the common ground when temperature is in tension.

### Vāta + Kapha — *warm, light-but-nourishing, well-spiced, not cold*
The trap: both are **cold**, so warmth serves both — but Vāta wants **moist/oily** and
Kapha wants **dry/light**, the opposite textures.
- **The overlap that serves both:** **warm and well-spiced.** Both are cold, so warmth
  and warming spices (ginger, cumin, black pepper, turmeric) pacify both — this is the
  easy common ground.
- **The texture tension:** keep it **warm and lightly nourishing** — cooked and warm
  (for Vāta) but **not heavy, greasy, or cold** (for Kapha). Use *moderate* oil and
  *generous* spice: enough oil/warmth to settle Vāta, enough lightness and spice to not
  bog Kapha.
- **Favor:** warm spiced soups and cooked grains, cooked vegetables, warm (not iced)
  drinks, ginger tea.
- **Avoid both errors:** ❌ **cold, raw, dry** food (aggravates Vāta) and ❌
  **heavy, oily, cold, dense, sweet** food (aggravates Kapha). Cold food is the worst
  call for this pair — it feeds *both*.

## Breath & movement by dosha (PROVISIONAL — the yoga layer)

> **Vanessa's domain.** Draft from established yoga–Ayurveda synthesis, for her to
> correct and deepen. Note: precise *pranayama/asana → dosha* mappings come from the
> **yoga tradition & modern synthesis (esp. Frawley, *Yoga & Ayurveda*; Lad)**, NOT
> the ancient samhitas. Same governing law applies — **opposites pacify.**

**Vāta** (cold, mobile, dry, scattered) → *ground · warm · steady*
- Breath: **Nadi Shodhana** (alternate-nostril, steadying); gentle Ujjayi. Avoid fast/forceful breath.
- Move: slow, grounding, stable — forward folds, gentle standing poses, child's pose. Consistency over intensity.

**Pitta** (hot, sharp, intense) → *cool · calm · soften*
- Breath: **Sheetali / Sitkari** (cooling breaths); left-nostril / Chandra Bhedana (cooling).
- Move: cooling, non-competitive — easy twists, moon-toned practice. Avoid midday heat & overexertion. Surrender over striving.

**Kapha** (heavy, slow, cold, stable) → *stimulate · warm · lighten*
- Breath: **Kapalabhati / Bhastrika** (heating, energising); right-nostril / Surya Bhedana (heating).
- Move: vigorous, warming, lifting — sun salutations, backbends, flowing sequences. Build heat & momentum.

## Women's health — cycle-aware (PROVISIONAL)

> Distilled from **Kate O'Donnell, *Everyday Ayurveda for Women's Health***
> (copyrighted — paraphrased, cited, not copied). Vanessa's domain to confirm/deepen.
> **Not medical advice — the book itself repeatedly defers to a practitioner.**

The distinctive, *safe* frame: a woman's **needs shift across her cycle** — the doshas
take turns. This is **"your needs are not constant"** at the timescale of a month,
and it's the women's-health wedge: cycle-*aware*, never hormonal/medical claims.

- **Menstruation (~days 1–5):** **Vāta** leads — *apana vāyu*, the downward-moving
  current that governs the flow. Needs: rest, warmth, grounding, less exertion.
- **Building phase (~days 5–16, *rutukala*):** **Kapha** rises — building *rasa
  dhātu* (nourishing juices), the fertile, building time. Needs: nourishment,
  steadiness, more capacity for activity.
- **Premenstrual (~days 16–28, *rutavateetakala*):** **Pitta** rises — through
  *rakta dhātu*; the lining matures. Needs: cooling, calm, ease; watch heat/irritability.
- (The phases echo the moon's cycle — energetically, not by literal overlap.)

Life stages shift the dominant dosha too; **menopause is a Vāta-increasing transition.**
Key women's tonic herb: **Shatavari** (supports *artava*, the reproductive tissue/essence).

**Line we don't cross:** cycle-*aware* guidance (gentler vs. more energising by phase)
is safe and beautiful; hormonal or medical claims are not ours to make — defer to a
practitioner. (Product note: a gentle, optional cycle phase in the check-in could
make guidance feel remarkably personal — and it's Vanessa's authority to shape.)
