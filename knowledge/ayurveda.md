## The governing principle (this is the engine)

> **"Equal qualities lead to increase; opposing qualities lead to decrease."**
> — *AH Su. 1.13½*

Everything the app recommends flows from this one law. A quality you already have
*too much of* is aggravated by more of the same and **pacified by its opposite.**
Example from the text: a dry (Vata) person in dry, cold weather grows drier — and
oil softens it back. (*AH Su. 1.13½*)

So the honest logic is not "what's your type" but: **which qualities are running
high today → offer their opposites.** This is the real basis for "Ground / Cool /
Stir," and it replaces any arbitrary scoring.

## The three doshas

Three forces (*tridoṣa*): **Vāta, Pitta, Kapha**. *"Perfect balance of the three
leads to health; imbalance leads to disease."* (*AH Su. 1.5½–6½*)

### Vāta — the **wind** (air + ether)
Qualities (*guṇa*), *AH Su. 1.10½*: **rūkṣa** dry · **laghu** light · **śīta** cold ·
**khara** rough · **sūkṣma** subtle · **cala** moving/changeable.
- *Runs high as:* dryness, cold hands, restlessness, anxiety, irregular digestion/sleep, gas.
- *Pacify with the opposite:* warmth, moisture/oil, weight, steadiness, routine.

### Pitta — the **fire** (fire + a little water)
Qualities, *AH Su. 1.11*: **sasneha** slightly oily · **tīkṣṇa** sharp/penetrating ·
**uṣṇa** hot · **laghu** light · **sara** flowing · **drava** liquid.
- *Runs high as:* heat, sharpness, irritability, inflammation, loose/burning digestion.
- *Pacify with the opposite:* cooling, calm, sweetness, shade, less spice & intensity.

### Kapha — the **earth** (earth + water)
Qualities, *AH Su. 1.12*: **snigdha** oily · **śīta** cold · **guru** heavy ·
**manda** slow/mild · **ślakṣṇa** smooth · **sthira** stable.
- *Runs high as:* heaviness, sluggishness, congestion, lethargy, slow digestion, holding on.
- *Pacify with the opposite:* lightness, warmth, stimulation, movement, less heavy/sweet.

*(The 20 guṇas come in ten opposed pairs — heavy/light, slow/sharp, cold/hot,
oily/dry, smooth/rough, solid/liquid, soft/hard, stable/mobile, subtle/gross,
non-slimy/slimy — AH Su. 1.18. This pairing is the whole toolkit.)*

## Prakriti vs Vikriti — nature vs weather

- **Prakriti** (constitution): inherent from conception, set by the qualities of
  sperm & ovum; single-dosha, dual-dosha, or balanced (*sama*, considered best).
  (*AH Su. 1.9–10*) — **fixed; not honestly pinned by a quick quiz.**
- **Vikriti** (current state): the *imbalance showing up now*. This is what daily
  check-ins read.

This is the textual ground for our **"weather, not labels"** stance: lead with
Vikriti (today), let Prakriti emerge slowly.

## Agni — the digestive fire

Four states (*AH Su. 1.7½*):
- **Viṣama** (Vāta) — irregular: appetite high then low.
- **Tīkṣṇa** (Pitta) — sharp: strong, sometimes burning, appetite.
- **Manda** (Kapha) — dull: low, slow digestion.
- **Sama** — balanced: appetite and digestion steady, at the right time.

Agni is the doorway concept for *digestion* guidance — introduced in context, not up front.

## The doshas move through the day (validates time-awareness)

The doshas dominate by part of the day, life, and digestion (*AH Su. 1.6½–7½*):
- **Day & night:** first part **Kapha** (morning), middle **Pitta** (midday), last **Vāta** (evening).
- **Life:** childhood Kapha, adulthood Pitta, old age Vāta.
- **After eating:** Kapha → Pitta → Vāta across digestion.

→ Time-aware guidance isn't a gimmick; it's classical. A morning check-in and an
evening one can honestly differ.

## Taste (rasa) → dosha — for food guidance

Six tastes (*AH Su. 1.13½–14*): sweet (*madhura*), sour (*amla*), salt (*lavaṇa*),
bitter (*tikta*), pungent (*kaṭu*), astringent (*kaṣāya*).
- **Sweet, sour, salt** → pacify **Vāta**, increase **Kapha**.
- **Bitter, pungent, astringent** → pacify **Kapha**, increase **Vāta**.
- **Astringent, bitter, sweet** → pacify **Pitta**; sour, salt, pungent increase it.

(Potency *vīrya*: astringent/bitter/sweet tend cooling; sour/salt/pungent tend heating. *AH Su. 1*)

## Breath & movement by dosha (PROVISIONAL — the yoga layer)

> **Vanessa's domain.** Draft from established yoga–Ayurveda synthesis, for her to
> correct and deepen. Note: precise *pranayama/asana → dosha* mappings come from the
> **yoga tradition & modern synthesis (esp. Frawley, *Yoga & Ayurveda*; Lad)**, NOT
> the ancient samhitas. Same governing law applies — **opposites pacify.**

**Vāta** (cold, mobile, dry, scattered) → *ground · warm · steady*
- Breath: **Nadi Shodhana** (alternate-nostril, steadying); gentle Ujjayi. Avoid fast/forceful breath.
- Move: slow, grounding, stable — forward folds, gentle standing poses, child's pose. Consistency over intensity.

**Pitta** (hot, sharp, intense) → *cool · calm · soften*
- Breath: **Sheetali / Sitkari** (cooling breaths); left-nostril / Chandra Bhedana (cooling).
- Move: cooling, non-competitive — easy twists, moon-toned practice. Avoid midday heat & overexertion. Surrender over striving.

**Kapha** (heavy, slow, cold, stable) → *stimulate · warm · lighten*
- Breath: **Kapalabhati / Bhastrika** (heating, energising); right-nostril / Surya Bhedana (heating).
- Move: vigorous, warming, lifting — sun salutations, backbends, flowing sequences. Build heat & momentum.

_(Already reflected in the app's "Breath" and "Move" guidance lines — this is the source layer behind them.)_

## Women's health — cycle-aware (PROVISIONAL)

> Distilled from **Kate O'Donnell, *Everyday Ayurveda for Women's Health***
> (copyrighted — paraphrased, cited, not copied). Vanessa's domain to confirm/deepen.
> **Not medical advice — the book itself repeatedly defers to a practitioner.**

The distinctive, *safe* frame: a woman's **needs shift across her cycle** — the doshas
take turns. This is **"your needs are not constant"** at the timescale of a month,
and it's the women's-health wedge: cycle-*aware*, never hormonal/medical claims.

- **Menstruation (~days 1–5):** **Vāta** leads — *apana vāyu*, the downward-moving
  current that governs the flow. Needs: rest, warmth, grounding, less exertion.
- **Building phase (~days 5–16, *rutukala*):** **Kapha** rises — building *rasa
  dhātu* (nourishing juices), the fertile, building time. Needs: nourishment,
  steadiness, more capacity for activity.
- **Premenstrual (~days 16–28, *rutavateetakala*):** **Pitta** rises — through
  *rakta dhātu*; the lining matures. Needs: cooling, calm, ease; watch heat/irritability.
- (The phases echo the moon's cycle — energetically, not by literal overlap.)

Life stages shift the dominant dosha too; **menopause is a Vāta-increasing transition.**
Key women's tonic herb: **Shatavari** (supports *artava*, the reproductive tissue/essence).

**Line we don't cross:** cycle-*aware* guidance (gentler vs. more energising by phase)
is safe and beautiful; hormonal or medical claims are not ours to make — defer to a
practitioner. (Product note: a gentle, optional cycle phase in the check-in could
make guidance feel remarkably personal — and it's Vanessa's authority to shape.)
