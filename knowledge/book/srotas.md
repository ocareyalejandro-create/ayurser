# What We Are Made Of — The Channels (Srotas)

*Authored in our own voice, grounded in the classical
texts (public domain), informed by the teachers we credit in the References. We
learned from them; we did not copy them.*

## What they are

If the tissues are what the body is *made of*, the channels are how the body
*moves it around*. *Srotas* are the passages — visible and invisible — through
which everything in us flows: food and water, breath and blood, each tissue's
nourishment, the wastes on their way out, and even thought and feeling.

The classical view is sweeping and beautiful: the body is, in essence, a system
of channels. Wherever something moves from one place to another, there is a
channel carrying it. The texts say there are as many kinds of channel as there
are kinds of thing that flow — too many to count — and then name the great ones
worth knowing.

## The great channels

The principal *srotas* fall into natural groups:

- **The channels that bring the world in** — those carrying **breath** (*prāṇa*),
  **water and hydration** (*udaka*), and **food** (*anna*). Life enters here.
- **The seven tissue channels** — one feeding each *dhātu* in turn (plasma,
  blood, muscle, fat, bone, marrow, reproductive essence). These are the supply
  lines of the body's own building, the chain of the last chapter seen as
  *flow*.
- **The channels that carry the world out** — those for **urine**, **faeces**,
  and **sweat**. What the body finishes with, it must be free to release.
- **The channels of the deep and the subtle** — in women, the channel of
  **menstruation** (and, the texts add, of breast-milk); and the channels of the
  **mind** (*mano-vaha*), through which thought and feeling move.

To picture a person this way — as a living network of rivers and roads — is
already to understand most of how they fall ill and how they heal.

## The four ways a channel fails

Here the classical texts are remarkably precise, and it is worth learning,
because nearly every disturbance is one of just four (the *srotoduṣṭi*):

1. **Blockage (*saṅga*)** — the flow is obstructed, dammed. Things back up.
   Constipation, congestion, stiffness, stagnation, the heavy stuck feeling.
2. **Excess flow (*atipravṛtti*)** — the channel runs too freely, leaks, pours.
   Diarrhoea, sweating too much, bleeding, a discharge that won't stop.
3. **Pooling or dilation (*sira-granthi*)** — the channel swells, knots, balloons
   where flow gathers and will not pass cleanly. Growths, lumps, varicosities.
4. **Wrong passage (*vimārga-gamana*)** — the flow breaks its banks and goes
   where it should not, into a channel not meant for it. Things ending up in the
   wrong place entirely.

Read this way, "disease" becomes legible: something is blocked, or leaking, or
pooling, or astray. And most often, in the Ayurvedic eye, it begins with the
first of these — *blockage* — and with one familiar culprit.

## The great blocker — āma

We met *āma* in the chapter on the fire: the cold, sticky residue of food the
fire could not finish. This is where it does its damage. **Āma is what clogs the
channels.** It settles in the passages, narrows them, dulls their flow, and
starves whatever lies downstream. In the classical view, a vast share of illness
is simply this — good channels gummed by undigested residue — which is why
Ayurveda returns, again and again, to the fire. Clear the *āma*, and the channels
clear; clear the channels, and the body remembers how to flow.

## Keeping the channels open

The care is mostly the care we already know, seen from a new angle:

- **Tend the fire** so little *āma* is made — the root of clear channels.
- **Move.** Stagnation is a blocked channel; motion, warmth and a little spice
  are flow restored. The body is a system of rivers, and rivers want to run.
- **Let the wastes go freely** — never suppress the natural urges; a held urge is
  a channel told to dam itself, and the texts are stern about it.
- **And the mind's channels too.** Held feeling — swallowed grief, unspoken anger
  — blocks the *mano-vaha srotas* as surely as *āma* blocks the gut. Emotional
  flow is part of physical health; what is felt and released does not pool.

## In a breath

You are a system of channels, and health is free flow. Most trouble is one of
four — blocked, leaking, pooling, or astray — and most often it is *blockage*,
from undigested residue. Tend the fire, keep moving, let what must leave you
leave, and let feeling flow as freely as breath. A body that flows is a body at
ease.

## The classical voice

The texts hold that the whole person is woven of channels, and that to read a
disease is to ask of some channel: is it blocked, does it leak, does it pool,
has it gone astray? Health, in this light, is nothing more mysterious than
everything moving where it should, at the pace it should. *(After the Caraka
Saṃhitā, Vimānasthāna, on the srotas — rendered in our own words.)*
