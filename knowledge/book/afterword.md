# An Author's Note

*The closing note — on how this book was made, and the hands it must defer to.*

---

A word, at the end, about what you have just read and how to hold it.

## How this book was written

It was written in one voice, by us. We went to the source — the public-domain
classical texts, the *Caraka Saṃhitā*, the *Suśruta Saṃhitā*, and the *Aṣṭāṅga
Hṛdaya* — and quoted them where they speak best. And we learned from the modern
teachers named in each chapter's references — Lad, Frawley, Svoboda, and others —
as one learns from good teachers: by understanding, and then saying it in one's
own words. We have not reproduced their work. The synthesis, the order, and the
voice are ours; the gratitude is theirs.

## A living art, not a final word

Here is the honesty this book rests on, kept for the end so it could not be
mistaken for doubt at the beginning:

Ayurveda is a *living art*, not a fixed doctrine. The classical texts themselves
differ. Skilled teachers differ. A tradition two thousand years alive is a long
conversation, not a single verdict — and anyone who tells you otherwise is
selling certainty, which is the one thing a living medicine does not offer.

So this book holds the **convergent ground** — what the lineage broadly agrees
on, the teachings that have held across centuries and teachers — and where the
art genuinely varies, it tries to say so plainly rather than pretend. We would
rather be *honest* than appear *certain*. That honesty is not a weakness in the
book; it is the most trustworthy thing about it.

## The last word belongs to a person

And this, above all. A book can teach you the language and the patterns. It can
help you notice how you are and find your way gently back toward balance. It
cannot examine you, diagnose you, or treat what is wrong — and it should never
pretend to. The deepest source of truth in this medicine has always been a *real
practitioner*, with you in front of them, reading what no book can read.

So take this as it is meant: lifestyle wisdom, an invitation to pay attention to
your own life — never medical advice, never a diagnosis, never a substitute for
care. When something needs a healer, let it send you to one. The book is the map;
a person is the territory; and the territory always has the final say.

## A living book

Like its subject, this book is alive. It will grow, deepen, and be revised, and
it will be the better for every careful eye — a practitioner's, and yours. Read it
slowly, argue with it where you must, and keep what serves you.

*Take your time. Do it right.*
