# The Qualities (Guṇa)

informed by the teachers we credit in the References. We learned from them; we

## The most useful idea in the book

If you take only one thing from these pages, take this chapter. It is the
simplest idea in Ayurveda and, quietly, the most powerful — and you already use
it every day without a name for it.

The five elements express themselves as *qualities* — *guṇa* — and qualities are
things you can actually feel. Not "you have too much air," but: *I feel dry. I
feel cold. I feel heavy. I feel restless.* Ayurveda names these in pairs of
opposites. The classical list runs to ten pairs; these are the ones you will use
most:

- **Cold** and **Hot**
- **Dry** and **Oily** (moist)
- **Heavy** and **Light**
- **Dull** (slow) and **Sharp**
- **Stable** (settled) and **Mobile** (restless)

That is very nearly the whole working vocabulary. A morning, a meal, a mood, a
season — each can be read as a handful of these qualities.

## The one law

And here is what to do with them — the single law beneath all of Ayurvedic
practice:

> *Like increases like; opposites lead to decrease.*
> — *Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya*, Sūtrasthāna 1.13½

A quality grows when you add more of the same, and settles when you meet it with
its opposite. Cold food on a cold day deepens the cold; warmth answers it. A dry,
restless mind is not calmed by a dry cracker and a cold wind — it is calmed by
warmth, oil, and weight. Heaviness is not lifted by more rest but by movement and
light. *Whatever is running high, reach for its opposite.* That is most of the
art, in a sentence.

## Why qualities matter more than labels

This is also why you do not need to know your "type" to begin. Much is made of
the three doshas — and we will meet them next — but the doshas are only *bundles
of these qualities given names*. You can practise Ayurveda for years through the
qualities alone, simply by reading today's weather in yourself and answering it:

*Am I dry or oily today? Hot or cold? Heavy or light? Restless or settled?* — and
then leaning, gently, toward the opposite of whatever is loud.

This is exactly how this whole app reads you. It never tries to pin down who you
*are*; it notices how you *are*, today, in qualities, and offers their opposites.
Weather, not a label — and the qualities are the language of the weather.

## The bridge

Two short steps remain before the forces. The **three forces** (next) are just
familiar bundles of these qualities — the dry-cold-light-mobile bundle, the
hot-sharp bundle, the heavy-cold-oily bundle. And the **six tastes** are these
same qualities made edible — the warming, the cooling, the lightening, the
grounding — so that food becomes the most everyday medicine of all. Learn the
qualities, and both of those become easy.

## In a breath

Everything can be read as a few felt qualities — hot or cold, dry or oily, heavy
or light, sharp or dull, restless or settled. And everything is met by the one
law: *like increases like; opposites restore balance.* Read what is running high;
reach for its opposite. That is Ayurveda in your own two hands.
