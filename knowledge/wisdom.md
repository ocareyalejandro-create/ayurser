# Wisdom — cited classical lines for the app's "warm doorways"

A small, curated collection of **public-domain classical verses** we may set, quietly,
in the app — a line of ancestral wisdom beside the guidance. Per `PHILOSOPHY.md`
("real words, warm doorways") and the lines we don't cross (we cite; we do not redistribute).

**Rules for this file:**
- Only **public-domain classical sources** (the saṃhitās) go into the app *verbatim*, cited.
- **Modern authors (Sebastian Pole, Lad, Frawley) are admired but NOT reproduced** —
  paraphrase and attribute; never lift their prose onto a screen.
- **Verify every attribution against the source before it appears in the app.** A
  beautifully-attributed *wrong* citation is exactly the dishonesty we avoid. Lines
  marked *(verify)* are not yet confirmed to a chapter and verse.

---

## The governing law — *already in use, confirmed*
> *Like increases like; opposites restore balance.*

— **Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya, Sūtrasthāna 1.13½.** The rasa/guṇa law the engine runs on.

## On the healthy person — *svastha*
> *That person who always eats wholesome food, keeps a regular lifestyle, remains
> unattached to the objects of the senses, gives and forgives, loves truth, and
> serves others — is without disease.*

— **Aṣṭāṅga Hṛdaya Saṃhitā** *(verse to verify)*. The wording above is **Sebastian
Pole's English rendering** — admired, but for display we use a public-domain
translation or our own, never his words.

*Why it matters:* this is the soul-line. Health as **whole-life harmony** — diet,
rhythm, non-attachment, ethics, service — not diet alone. The classical *svastha*
("established in oneself"). Cross-reference **Suśruta Sū. 15.41** (*samadoṣa
samāgni...*), the canonical definition of health, *(verify and add)*.

## On food before medicine
> *When diet is wrong, medicine is of no use. When diet is correct, medicine is of no need.*

— **Traditional Ayurvedic aphorism.** Widely hung on Charaka's name, but **not verbatim
in the Charaka Saṃhitā** — so attribute it as *"traditional,"* or trace the true source,
before it ever goes on a screen. Love the line; don't fake its lineage.
